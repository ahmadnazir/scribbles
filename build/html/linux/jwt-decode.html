Decode a JWTÂ¶

Assuming that the jwt is in the token.json file:

cat token.json | cut -d"." -f2 | base64 --decode -i | jq "."

Or create an alias:

alias jwt-decode='cut -d"." -f2 - | base64 --decode -i | jq "."'
cat token.json | jwt-decode
